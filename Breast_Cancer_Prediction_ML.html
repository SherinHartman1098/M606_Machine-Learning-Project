Breast Cancer Prediction

Problem Statement

The aim of this pipeline is to create a predictive model for breast cancer diagnosis using past medical data, addressing the crucial business issue of improving the breast cancer diagnostic procedure. Reducing mortality, increasing patient outcomes, and guaranteeing prompt intervention all depend on early and precise diagnosis. Resolving this issue improves reputation, reduces costs, increases operational efficiency, and improves diagnosis accuracy for healthcare providers. Patient demographics, clinical test findings, past medical records, and treatment outcomes are all part of the data collection process. Data preparation, feature selection, model training, hyperparameter tuning, and model evaluation are all steps in the machine learning task that lead to the deployment of a dependable model that is integrated into the diagnostic workflow. By offering a reliable instrument for prompt and accurate breast cancer diagnosis, this strategy hopes to improve patient outcomes in the long run.

Data Exploration (EDA)

Note: For EDA and data pre-processing, the entire dataset will be taken into consideration. Once the dataset is cleaned,it will be split into training and testing data-sets. And why this? Here's the reason:

  • Consistency: If only the test data is cleaned, the training data may still contain unwanted or redundant elements, leading to inconsistent and unreliable model performance.
  • Generalization: Cleaning the whole dataset guarantees the data is generalised, forestalling issues during both training and testing stages.
  • Robustness: This approach makes a strong model that performs reliably across various data subsets.

Conclusion and Discussion

Let's summarize how the Breast Cancer Prediction pipeline is designed to accurately classify and predict the presence of breast cancer using machine learning models.

The pipeline created with the Breast Cancer Prediction dataset, aims to achieve primary goal to accuratley classify if the breast tumor is benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous) based on various features extracted . It essentially, aims to build models that maximize predictive accuracy. Here are some fundamental steps that are carried out in th pipeline as follows:
